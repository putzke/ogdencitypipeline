About the Project

A Century-Old Lifeline, Renewed

Ogden City is replacing a critical 36-inch diameter water transmission pipeline that conveys water from the City's wellfield adjacent to Pineview Reservoir, through Ogden Canyon, and to the City's 23rd Street reservoirs. This pipeline has served Ogden City well for nearly 100 years and needs to be replaced.

The project will be completed in multiple phases between 2025 and 2029. Ogden City has selected Sunrise Engineering as the design engineer for the project and Whitaker Construction as the CMGC contractor for the project.

As design and planning of the project proceeds, additional outreach will take place with residents of Ogden Canyon who will be impacted by this project. Information regarding anticipated timing for construction of the phases of the project will be provided on this website. If you have specific questions regarding the project, please feel free to reach out to our public involvement team using the contact information on this website.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I expect water service interruptions?

Brief interruptions are possible as the watermain traverses through the canyon. However, Ogden Canyon is primarily served by a separate 24-inch pipeline. The 36-inch pipeline carries water for larger trunkline purposes, so impacts to local service are expected to be minimal.

Will access to my property be restricted?

Depending on the alignment and right-of-way, it may be necessary to temporarily limit access at property access points. We will actively coordinate and notify residents well in advance of any such impacts.

How are you ensuring safety in construction zones?

Please use extra caution and stay clear of construction zones, especially areas with deep trenches. Anticipate increased noise, dust, and vibration near active work. Follow road closure signs and do not enter areas zoned off by cones, barrels, or barricades.

Why is the pipeline being replaced now?

The current pipeline loses approximately 1 million gallons of drinking water daily due to leaks. The $100M replacement is funded through EPA's WIFIA program, state revolving loans, and ARPA funding. Over its lifetime, the new pipeline will save enough water to fill Pineview Reservoir three times.

Press Release

Pineview Reservoir Pipeline Crossing

📄 Full Press Release — Pineview Reservoir Crossing Draft · Ogden City Department of Public Utilities · For Immediate Release [Late June / Early July 2026]
⬇ Download PDF

For media inquiries, please contact the Sunrise Engineering Public Involvement Team at 801-838-8365. Our team will assist in answering your inquiries and direct you to the appropriate Ogden City Communications contact to schedule press interviews. · View Media Q&A ↓

Ogden City has announced the start of construction for the critical reservoir crossing phase of the Ogden Canyon 36-Inch Water Transmission Pipeline Replacement Project. As part of this work, Pineview Reservoir water levels will be lowered this summer to allow crews to install a new parallel transmission pipeline alongside the existing pipeline at the bottom of the reservoir.

The existing pipeline has been in service for approximately 60 years beneath Pineview Reservoir and nearly 100 years through Ogden Canyon. It currently loses approximately one million gallons of treated drinking water per day and is reaching the end of its service life. Replacement is essential to protect water system reliability and prevent failures that could interrupt service to more than 120,000 residents throughout the region.

The distinction between what the drought is doing to Pineview Reservoir and what this project requires is important. Utah's 2025–2026 water year has produced the lowest statewide snowpack on modern record, and Pineview Reservoir is already projected to reach historically low levels this summer independent of this project. Rather than drawing from other regional water sources, the project team will take advantage of naturally lower drought-driven reservoir levels to complete the work — preserving storage elsewhere and minimizing disruption to the broader regional water system.

"[QUOTE PLACEHOLDER — Justin Anderson, Public Utilities Director, Ogden City. Suggested themes: community responsibility, infrastructure investment, once-in-a-generation timing, commitment to reliable water service.]"

— Justin Anderson, Director of Public Utilities, Ogden City

The project does not require draining Pineview Reservoir. Crews will lower water levels sufficiently to install the new pipeline alongside the existing one within the established right-of-way. The existing pipeline will remain in service during construction and will afterward be rehabilitated as a redundant backup line — giving water managers two independent transmission lines for the first time.

The project is being carried out in close coordination with federal and state water management partners. All reservoir release operations will be authorized and supervised in compliance with state and federal water rights law. Reservoir levels are expected to be restored naturally through snowmelt infiltration by February 2027.

Ogden City recognizes that lower reservoir levels will affect recreation at Pineview Reservoir this season. The City has been in active coordination with recreation stakeholders, marina operators, and the Pineview Reservoir Yacht Club since early 2026. Updated access maps and boat ramp conditions will be posted regularly at this project website.
